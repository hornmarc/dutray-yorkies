FAQs

Curious about Yorkshire Terriers?

We are knowledgeable and professional and strive to learn as much as we can about this beautiful and affectionate breed. If we haven’t answered your questions, please visit our contact page and send us a note.

Personality

Yorkies are enthusiastic, adventurous, energetic, brave, loyal, clever, affectionate, and packed with personality.

Points of Interest

  • The Yorkie was originally a working class family’s dog and later became popular during the Victorian Era.
  • Two purebred Yorkshire Terriers with recessive genes produced a spotted Yorkie, later known as the Biewer.
  • The breed is considered above average in intelligence.

Appearance

The show look has an ultra long, silky coat that parts along the spine. Many family pets wear a puppy cut for easier grooming.

Stats

  • Common name: Yorkie
  • Breed group: Terrier, Toy
  • Area of origin: Northern England
  • Date of origin: officially became its own distinct breed in 1870
  • Weight: not over 7 lbs
  • Life expectancy: 12–15 years

Yorkie Color

Yorkie puppies are born black and tan, then transition as they mature. Parti and colored Yorkies have especially beautiful markings.
